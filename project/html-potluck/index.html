Pumpkin Pie French Toast

Cook Time

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 25 minutes
  • Total Time: 40 minutes
  • Yields: 3 to 4

If you're like me and dream of pumpkin pie for breakfast, this French toast is about to make your mornings way better! Imagine all the cozy flavors of pumpkin pie wrapped up in the most perfect, golden, caramelized French toast with every bite. It’s the ultimate breakfast to kick off fall, and trust me, you’ll be making it on repeat!

Ingredients

FRENCH TOAST
  • 1 brioche loaf, cut into 1/2 to 1-inch thick slices
  • 3/4 cup half and half
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 tablespoon light brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice, more to taste
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, to cook the French toast

CINNAMON BROWN SUGAR BUTTER
  • 1 stick unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • pinch of salt
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar

FOR SERVING
  • powdered sugar
  • maple syrup

INSTRUCTIONS

MAKE THE CINNAMON BUTTER
  1. To make the cinnamon butter, combine unsalted butter, cinnamon, salt, brown sugar, and powdered sugar. Mix together until smooth and set aside.
MAKE THE FRENCH TOAST
  1. To a bowl, make the custard to dip the bread in. Combine half and half, eggs, pumpkin puree, brown sugar, vanilla extract, pumpkin pie spice, and cinnamon. Whisk really well until completely smooth and homogenous.
  2. Heat a large pan over medium heat. Add a little bit of butter. If the pan is too hot, the butter will burn so make sure it's not too high.
  3. Dip each brioche slice in the custard quickly and place on the pan. Repeat with a few more until the pan is full but not crowded.
  4. Cook for about 2 minutes, then turn each one over and repeat so every side of the roll gets golden brown and crispy. Take off the pan and repeat with the rest of the bread.
  5. Add a dollop of the cinnamon butter, sprinkle with powdered sugar, and drizzle maple syrup on top to enjoy!

Breakfast

Pumpkin Bagels

Cook Time

  • Prep Time: 2 hours
  • Cook Time: 45 minutes
  • Total Time: 3 hours
  • Yields: 8 to 10 bagels

Craving all the cozy fall vibes? These pumpkin bagels are everything you love about the season, packed into one chewy, flavorful bite. Trust me, whether you’re having them toasted with butter or piled high with cream cheese, they’re about to become your new go-to breakfast treat!

Ingredients

DOUGH:
  • 5 cups bread flour
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/3 cup dark brown sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 3/4 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon oil, for brushing the bowl

BOILING:
  • 8 to 10 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons malt syrup or molasses
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda

EGG WASH:
  • EGG WASH:
  • 1 tablespoon whole milk

INSTRUCTIONS

PREPARE THE DOUGH:
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, salt, brown sugar, pumpkin spice, and cinnamon. Whisk together to ensure the spices are evenly distributed throughout the flour mixture.
  2. In a separate small bowl, dissolve the active dry yeast in warm water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes, until it becomes foamy and activated.
  3. In the center of the flour mixture, create a well and add the pumpkin puree and the yeast mixture. Begin mixing the ingredients together, gradually incorporating the flour into the wet ingredients.
  4. Once the dough starts to come together, transfer it to a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes. The dough should become smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky to the touch. If it feels too sticky, add a bit more flour, a tablespoon at a time.
  5. Lightly brush a large bowl with oil, then place the dough in the bowl, turning it to coat the surface with oil. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free spot for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
SHAPING THE BAGELS:
  1. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Divide the dough into equal portions, depending on how large you want your bagels (about 10-12 pieces).
  2. Roll each portion into a long rope, about 8-10 inches in length. Then, take the ends of the rope and press them together to form a ring, making sure the ends are securely sealed. Place the shaped bagels on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and cover them with a towel.
  3. Let them rest for 15-20 minutes while you prepare the boiling water.
BOILING THE BAGELS:
  1. In a large pot, bring 8 to 10 cups of water to a boil. Add the malt syrup or molasses, salt, and baking soda to the boiling water, stirring until dissolved.
  2. Gently lower a few bagels at a time into the boiling water. Boil each bagel for about 1 minute on each side, using a slotted spoon to flip them over. The longer you boil, the chewier the bagels will be. Once boiled, remove the bagels from the water and place them back on the parchment-lined baking sheet.
EGG WASH & BAKING:
  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and whole milk to create an egg wash. Brush the top of each bagel with the egg wash.
  3. Bake the bagels in the preheated oven for 20-22 minutes, or until they are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.
COOLING:
  1. Once baked, remove the bagels from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack. Serve warm or at room temperature. Enjoy them plain, toasted, or with your favorite spread!

Breakfast

Pumpkin Churros

Cook Time

  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: 30 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hours
  • Yields: 10 to 12 bagels

Pumpkin season is here, and I had to turn one of my favorite fall flavors into something fun and delicious! These pumpkin-shaped churros are crispy, warm, and loaded with pumpkin spice goodness—basically fall in every bite. Trust me, you’ll want to make a batch because they’ll d isappear in seconds!

Ingredients

CINNAMON SUGAR MIXTURE
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

CHURRO BATTER
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • vegetable oil, for frying

OPTIONAL DIPPING SAUCES
  • caramel sauce
  • chocolate sauce
  • sweetened condensed milk

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together sugar and cinnamon to make the cinnamon-sugar mixture. Set aside.
  2. In a pot, add water, sugar, vanilla extract, pumpkin puree, unsalted butter, and salt.
  3. Set over medium-high heat and whisk together.
  4. Once it comes to a boil, drop the heat to low and add the flour. Use a rubber spatula to mix together until there are no more clumps of flour left, about 3 minutes.
  5. Take off the heat and allow to cool for 6-8 minutes.
  6. Add one egg and mix using a rubber spatula or in a stand mixer until fully incorporated. Add the second egg and mix again until fully incorporated. The dough should be firm, elastic and just a bit sticky.
  7. Transfer into a piping bag fitted with a tip.
  8. Cut parchment paper into small squares. Spray with oil and pipe pumpkins out onto each square. You may need to use scissors to cut the stem off at the end.
  9. Heat oil to 325°F and drop one heart in at a time, making sure to move slowly so it drops into the oil smoothly. Allow it to get golden brown on one side before flipping over. Once it’s golden on both sides, transfer to a paper towel to drain excess oil. Repeat until all the churros are fried.
  10. Coat the churros in the cinnamon sugar mixture.
  11. Enjoy with some caramel or chocolate sauce!
